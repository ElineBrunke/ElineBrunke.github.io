In the years 2020-2023 the corona virus spread all over the world, and the USA was no exception. This naturally lead to a large number of covid related hospitalizations, deaths and days with critically low staffing at the american hospitals. All this will be investigated further in this article, using several different data set from hospitals all across the states.

The spread of covid-19 throughout the US

The covid-19 pandemic had its seed in the early weeks of January in the US but really caught on throughout the late weeks of June and early July. We can observe in the heat map that the new cases after mid July frequently pops up in all states and gives a fine overview over exactly how quickly the pandemic grew.

The new cases of covid-19 over time, with the states indentified as either Democrat, Republican or Swing-state.

Throuout the entire pandemic, it can be observed that the disease spread has bigger clusters on the east coast. This is likely do to the states and bigger cities on the east coast being more densely populated than on the west coast. It is also because of this fact that it becomes more difficult to place the bigger outbreaks based on political governance, since thinly populated states take up much space on the map. Because of their low number of inhabitants, investigating the covid cases in regards to population could reveal new, interesting angles.

The effect of state density

To investigate and compare the effect of state density, the states was clustered into either high, mid or low density, based on the states populationnumbers. Then, when comparing the average hospitalised covid patients pr capita, the higher populated states surprisingly didn't seem to differ much from the mid and lower populated states and there were even months between June 21 and December 21, where it was lower than for the low and mid-populated states.

The number of hospitalised patients is refelcted in the critical staffing, which is shown to follow the trends for all the three density groups. However, while the average number of hospitalised covid patients pr capity was surprisingly similar, the average critical staffing rate for the high density states by far overtook the rates of the mid and low density states after December 2020, revealing way more pressure on the healthcare in high density states for the entire year of 2021.
